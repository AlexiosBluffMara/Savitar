## Key Narrative Elements

### Discord as IRC Successor
- IRC fostered meritocratic, pseudonymous, contribution-first communities
- Discord inherited this culture and made it accessible
- Both are free, channel-based, and bot-friendly
- Savitar uses Discord as the primary community surface

### Identity and Exclusivity for Academic Networks
- Verified identity (institutional email or vouched membership)
- Invitation-only gates for trust building
- Role-based access (students / faculty / community partners)
- Audit trails for academic integrity and grant reporting

### Three Messaging Streams
- Discord: global community (students, developers, orgs)
- iMessage: American consumers and professionals (native, encrypted, zero-friction)
- WhatsApp: international communities, immigrant business owners (2B+ users)
- One agent, one gateway, one web UI — three surfaces

### Budget Tiers
- Tier 0: $0, join existing server
- Tier 1: $100–300, Pi/old Mac, E2B
- Tier 2: $599–799, Mac Mini M4, E4B (sweet spot)
- Tier 3: $1,399–2,999, Mac Mini Pro/Mac Studio, full quality
- Tier 4: $20–100/mo, cloud VM
