# Community Narrative

## Why Discord is the right front door

Savitar works best as an invite-only Discord system because Discord already supports the trust pattern this project needs:

- gated membership,
- role-based channels,
- direct messages,
- files, links, and short video clips,
- operator-only spaces,
- simple access from phones and laptops.

That makes it a better first surface than a public website for the people this project is trying to help.

## The operating story

Alexios Bluff Mara LLC runs Savitar as a neighborhood-scale operator system for Chicago.

The target users are not abstract “AI consumers.” They are people with constrained time, constrained budgets, and real trust concerns:

- small-business owners navigating grants, filings, and vendor questions,
- educators coordinating live learning sessions and local materials,
- community organizers maintaining trusted knowledge spaces.

The point is not to give them another general chatbot. The point is to give them a local operator that understands their approved materials and behaves inside a trusted workflow.

## Educator Live Mode

Educator Live Mode is the cleanest education story in the repo.

- An educator opens a dedicated Discord channel for a class or workshop.
- Savitar answers from curated local packs.
- The operator console shows evidence and review state.
- The educator can step in, pin corrections, or reject a bad answer.

That maps directly to the competition’s education language around empowering the educator through multi-tool assistance.

## Local knowledge matters

Savitar should be framed around local knowledge packs stored on attached SSD:

- markdown notes,
- class materials,
- small-business operating docs,
- policy references,
- community resource guides,
- research notes and curated links.

This is a stronger and more honest story than “web-scale knowledge.” Judges can understand exactly what the system knows and why it knows it.

## Why this fits Digital Equity

- The hardware story is reachable.
- The interface is familiar.
- The operator can support multilingual and mixed-format workflows.
- Communities do not need to surrender every question to a remote hosted model.

Digital equity here is not abstract. It is a practical claim: more people can operate useful AI on hardware and channels they already understand.

## Why this fits Safety & Trust

- Invite-only access instead of anonymous public sprawl.
- Role-gated channels.
- Evidence-backed outputs.
- Operator review and override.
- Provenance and transparent guardrails.
- Honest failure modes when evidence is missing.

That is a stronger trust story than promising perfect answers.
